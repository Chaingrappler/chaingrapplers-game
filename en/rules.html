Rules

Learn the game fast, then check the full rules.

Start with the short version, then move into the exact chain logic, submission pressure, and special card effects.

Quick Rules

  1. Give the youngest player the Takedown card.
  2. Shuffle the deck and deal the cards.
  3. Each player starts with 6 cards.
  4. The round opens with Takedown.
  5. On your turn, you may play up to 2 cards.
  6. Most cards follow the chain. Your card's Start color must match the current card's Result color.
  7. If a player attacks, the opponent must answer with a matching defensive card.
  8. At the end of your turn, you may draw 1 card unless you are holding Fatigue.
  9. If a submission is active, the defender must escape. With no playable card, they get one Hail Mary draw. If it is not playable, the defender is submitted.

How to Read a Card

Each positional card shows a move from one position into another.

  • Start color: where the move begins.
  • Result color: where the move ends.
  • Green: neutral exchange.
  • Yellow: stronger pressure and control.
  • Orange: major advantage and finishing pressure.
  • Red: submission threat or submission escape.

Full Rules

Setup

  • The game is for 2 players.
  • Shuffle the deck and deal 6 cards to each player.
  • One player opens the round with Takedown.

Legal cards and turns

  • Any defensive card is legal to play on Takedown.
  • After that, a card is legal when its start color matches the current result color.
  • If a player attacks, the opponent must defend.
  • If a player defends, the next response is offensive.
  • You may play up to 2 cards on your turn, then draw 1 card unless Fatigue blocks drawing.

Submissions and Hail Mary

  • A red offensive card creates an active submission threat.
  • The defender must answer with an escape.
  • If the defender has no playable escape, they draw one Hail Mary card.
  • If the Hail Mary card is an escape, play continues. If not, the defender loses by submission.

Special Cards

  • Mat Enforcer: draw 3 cards.
  • Teenage Rage: the next card may be any follow-up.
  • The Black Belt: pick up the most recently played card, except Takedown.
  • Fatigue: blocks drawing while it is in your hand.
  • Ultra Heavy: steal 1 random card from your opponent.

Winning

  • Submission: win when the opponent cannot answer a submission.
  • Card advantage: if the deck is exhausted and the chain stops, fewer cards left wins.
  • Draw: if both players are equally stuck, the round is a draw.
