Rules

Learn the game fast, then check the full rules.

Start with the short version, then move into the exact chain logic, submission pressure, and special card effects.

Quick Rules

  1. Give the youngest player the Takedown card.
  2. Shuffle the deck and deal the cards.
  3. Each player starts with 6 cards.
  4. The round opens with Takedown.
  5. On your turn, you may play up to 2 cards.
  6. Most cards follow the chain. Your card's Start color (top) must match the current card's Result color (bottom).
  7. If a player plays an offensive card, the opponent must answer with a defensive card that matches the color.
  8. At the end of your turn, you may draw 1 card (if you are not holding Fatigue, which prevents you from drawing new cards).
  9. If a submission is active, the defender must answer with an escape. If they have no playable card, they get one Hail Mary draw from the pile. If the Hail Mary card is not playable on the submission, the defender has been submitted.

How to Read a Card

Each positional card shows a move from one position into another.

  • Start color (top): where the move begins.
  • Result color (bottom): where the move ends.

Think of every normal card as a positional transition.

  • Green: the most neutral state. Momentum can swing here.
  • Yellow: stronger pressure and stronger control.
  • Orange: major advantage and high finishing pressure.
  • Red: submission threat or submission escape.
  • Defensive cards pull the exchange back toward near-neutral half guard.

Full Rules

Setup

  • The game is for 2 players.
  • Shuffle the deck and deal 6 cards to each player (the Takedown card should be one of them).
  • One player opens the round with Takedown (must be played alone.).

How cards become legal

  • Any defensive card is legal to play on Takedown.
  • After that, a card is legal when its Start color (top) matches the current card's Result color (bottom).
  • If a player plays an offensive card, the opponent must answer with a defensive card that matches the color.
  • If a player plays a defensive card, the next response is offensive.
  • If an attacker still has a second legal offensive card, they may continue the attack on the same turn.

Turn structure

  • You may play up to 2 cards on your turn.
  • At the end of your turn, you may draw 1 card (if you are not holding Fatigue, which prevents you from drawing new cards).
  • You may choose to stop and draw even if you still have a legal move.
  • If Fatigue is in your hand, drawing is blocked. You must either play a legal card, or end the turn without drawing.

Submissions

  • A red offensive card creates an active submission threat.
  • When you are under submission, you may not draw, pass, or end turn unless the Hail Mary rule triggers.
  • You must answer with an escape.
  • If you have no playable card after a red submission is played, draw one Hail Mary card from the pile.
  • If the Hail Mary card is an escape card, play it immediately and continue as normal. If it is not an escape card, you are submitted and lose immediately.

Special Cards

  • Mat Enforcer: draw 3 cards. It counts as one played card. The chain does not change, and the card is thrown into the discard pile.
  • Teenage Rage: play it into the chain. The next card may be any follow-up.
  • The Black Belt: play it into the chain, then pick up the most recently played card, except Takedown. The next card may be any follow-up.
  • Fatigue: can only be played as your first and only card of the turn. After Fatigue, the next player may play any card.
  • Ultra Heavy cards: steal 1 random card from your opponent. The chain does not change, and the card is thrown into the discard pile.

Winning

  • Submission: if your opponent cannot answer an active submission and fails the Hail Mary draw, you win.
  • Card advantage: if the deck is exhausted and the chain can no longer continue, the player with fewer cards left wins.
  • Draw: if both players are equally stuck, the round is a draw.
